## About the project
This project is dedicated to our small dog, Hazel. 

On September 27 2025, Hazel was having what we thought would be a normal day, but on a sunny afternoon, she collapsed after running and fell in the yard. We never leave her alone outside, so we rushed to her side when we heard what we'd describe as haunting whines for help. Initially we thought it was a seizure, but as time dragged on, her eyes turned lifeless.

We genuinely thought we lost her. In an out-of-body experience, we gave compressions in disbelief.

After what could only be described as impossibly hour-long minutes, she dopily came back.

Perplexed, we rushed to the emergency vet. We were anxiously hoping she'd survive the drive, yet weirdly she seemed so normal. It was almost as if she had no clue what just happened.

Several tests later, we'd learn that she had been silently coping with Congestive Heart Failure. We didn't recognize the signs, but the imaging showed an enlarged heart nearly shutting her trachea and fluid in the lungs-- pulmonary edema. A murmur could be heard as though a loud engine was thundering in her enlarged barrel chest.

After a long visit to the emerge, we were sent home with a short six month prognosis, and a prescription for what would become the start of many managing medications matching a diagnosis of late stage mitral valve disease that's progressed to congestive heart failure.

This project tracks Hazel's CHF journey and shares personal insight from a caregiver's perspective. The naive in me hopes no one goes through the same, but I know how common it is. I'm only hopeful that this approachable and non-clinical information can give you some peace through your journey.

## About Hazel
Hazel is a 10 year old rescue chihuahua min-pin. That's what her DNA test will tell you, but we think she's part dachshund too. Whatever she is, she's the sweetest soul.

![Picture of Hazel being camera shy](/images/hazel-shy.png)

Her timid and sometimes shy personality can be heartbreaking. She was rescued from a house in Niagara which had hoarded over 150 small dogs. From what we read and what we're told, the conditions were terrible. Provincial Animal Welfare Services (PAWS) was able to save and disperse most of the dogs.

![Picture of Hazel being rescued from Niagara](/images/hazel-paws.png)

Hazel is pictured being removed from the home in this crate. She's the small brown dog, just barely in the picture. With her is her good friend Cricket.

We came upon Hazel during Covid and she quickly found her role as the princess of our house.

![Picture of Hazel relaxing in the sun inside](/images/hazel-gotcha.png)

She immediately became a velcro dog to my wife. We were warned that she prefers females and is afraid of men, so for a while, she wasn't so sure of me.

Over time, I'd like to think that changed.

![Picture of Hazel sleeping close to me](/images/hazel-close.png)
